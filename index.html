Background In Mini Racer, the background was an image of the device that i was inspired. My objective is to put the canvas 'as screen' of the device, i've made this time just positioning the canvas hardcoded over screen part of the image. 
<img src="minigame.png" style="position: absolute; z-index: -1; width: 280px;" align="center">
Since this background is an image and the canvas has to be on top of it, i've set on the attribute z-index a value lower than the z-index of the canvas. The screens on this type of device are always some kind of green, to archive that i've painted the canva's background with color-like to match the original screen color. 
<canvas id="miniRacerCanvas" width="100" height="150" tabindex="1" 
	style="position: relative; left: 86px; top: 46px; border:1px solid #d3d3d3; background: #B8C7AD;">
	Your browser does not support the HTML5 canvas tag.
</canvas>
If the browser is too old or don't support HTML5's canvas, an message will be displayed positioned where the canvas should be.
